Taking into account the climate, orientation, and shading, in the results below we have assessed the average seasonal
(winter / summer) solar radiation falling upon the windows in the project. The radiation levels will vary by orientation
and as can be observed, shading obstructions also have a strong effect on the final level of solar radiation any
individual window receives.

- Very good solar radiation is observed along the south facade which will help to reduce the wintertime heating energy
  need significantly.
- Very limited solar gain observed on all north side glazing. However thanks to the design, the north side is not overly glazed 
and so we do not recommend any change at this point.
- While the high levels of solar gain on the south do help reduce heating energy demand, it is possible that at some
  times this solar gain may be unwanted. It is recommended to ensure that all south-facing glazing are outfitted with robust 
  user-operable blinds / shades to ensure against any thermal discomfort. While external shades are preferred, internal shades 
  are a good option as well and can still help with glare and overheating issues.
