This resilience assessment uses hourly EnergyPlus outputs prepared through the Honeybee-REVIVE workflow and evaluates
the thermal conditions during simulated loss of utility service. The charts retain the full 240-hour simulation record
and shade the declared 168-hour (seven-day) outage interval. The first 24 hours precede the outage and the final 48 hours
follow it.

The criteria shown here follow **Phius REVIVE 2024 v24.1.1**. They are reported as design-analysis results, not as a
Phius certification determination. Each result is limited to what the staged source series can prove:

- the Summer chart evaluates hours in the Heat Index Danger and Extreme Danger categories, but does not calculate the
  separate Mora deadly-day criterion;
- the Winter chart evaluates SET degree-hours below 54°F (12.22°C), but does not infer the separate zero-hours-below-36°F
  air-temperature criterion from SET.

The exact CSVs used by the charts are retained as report downloads, together with source-simulation hashes and generator
versions in the Resilience sidecar manifest.

<div class="btwr-callout btwr-callout--critical">
  <strong>Design implication</strong>
  <span>
    The Summer Heat Index criterion does not pass in the current simulation. Passive solar control, night ventilation,
    and any on-site-powered cooling strategy should be revised and re-simulated before treating the design as thermally
    resilient during the modeled extreme-week outage.
  </span>
</div>
