For assessing the winter resiliency, the **Phius REVIVE** protocol uses the [Standard Effective Temperature (SET)](https://bigladdersoftware.com/epx/docs/9-5/engineering-reference/resilience-metrics.html#set-hour) metric 
in order to evaluate zone habitability during the stress-period. In particular, to comply with the **Phius REVIVE** standard, 
it is required that all zones exhibit:

- 0 hours below 2 °C [36°F] **SET**
- < 216 °F-Hours below 12 °C [54°F] **SET**

<div class="btwr-callout">
  <strong>Result</strong>
  <span>
    As shown below, by virtue of the exceptional well insulated envelope, high quality glazing, and good airtightness - 
this shows zone interior temperatures dropping below 14°C [57 °F] only after 4 days of power-outage. This very good thermal performance shows 
that the home would be safe during an extended period of severe weather, and even without any active heating of any 
form. The home, in particular the bedroom level in the North wing, do see low temperatures by the 6th and 7th day of the power outage, but still do not
fall below 4° C [39.2 °F]. Based on these results, it does not appear that any modifications are recommended at this time.
